Welcome to the CAN-ACT Study

New cannabis cultivation and possession laws came into effect in the Australian Capital Territory (ACT) in January 2020. These allow ACT residents to grow and possess small quantities of cannabis. Our research team from the Lambert Initiative at the University of Sydney are conducting this survey of people residing in the ACT to examine their experiences around these new laws.

Eligibility
You are invited to participate in the study, if:
  • You are an adult who currently lives in the ACT; and
  • You have used and/or cultivated cannabis or cannabis products since these laws came into effect
The CAN-ACT study involves two components:
  1. An online survey
  2. The opportunity (if you wish) to have your cannabis samples analysed for cannabinoid content (i.e. THC, CBD and other cannabinoids) and possible contaminants (e.g. pesticides, heavy metals)

The survey asks confidentially about:
  • You: your age, gender, level of education and current living and work situations
  • Your history of cannabis use
  • Your experience with growing cannabis, if any
  • Your use of prescribed and/or non-prescribed cannabis products for medical purposes, if any
  • Any side effects that you may have experienced when using cannabis
  • Your opinions of the current legislation in ACT around cannabis
The survey should take around 10 minutes to complete.

Your answers are anonymous and confidential. You will be allocated an anonymised user ID code that in no way allows your responses to be matched to personal and/or identifying data. We do not ask for any information that might identify you.

Please keep note of your user ID and password. As the survey is anonymised, the research team will be unable to locate or reset your login details.

Once you complete the survey, you can register to have a sample of your cannabis analysed for cannabinoid and contaminant content.
