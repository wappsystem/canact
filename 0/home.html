Welcome to the CAN-ACT Study


New cannabis cultivation and possession laws came into effect in the Australian Capital Territory (ACT) in January 2020. These allow ACT residents to grow and possess small quantities of cannabis.

Our research team from the Lambert Initiative at the University of Sydney are conducting this survey to understand ACT residents’ experiences around the new laws, including how you think they can be improved.



The CAN-ACT study involves

  1. An anonymous online survey (~10 mins)
  2. The opportunity (if you wish) to have your cannabis samples analysed for cannabinoid content (i.e. THC, CBD and other cannabinoids) and possible contaminants (e.g. pesticides, heavy metals), anonymously and free-of-charge

Legality, Privacy, and Anonymity

  • ACT and NSW Governments have authorised participants to provide cannabis samples to the CAN-ACT study team
  • The survey does not ask any information that could identify you or collect your IP address or your pickup address
  • The courier will not ask your name, only your pickup address (which doesn’t need to be your home)
  • The study courier is contractually bound to destroy the pickup address after collection


Eligibility
You are invited to participate in the study, if:

  • You are an adult who currently lives in the ACT; and
  • You have used and/or cultivated cannabis or cannabis products since these laws came into effect

The survey asks confidentially about:

  • You: your age, gender, level of education and current living and work situations
  • Your history of cannabis use
  • Your experience with growing cannabis, if any
  • Your use of prescribed and/or non-prescribed cannabis products for medical purposes, if any
  • Any side effects that you may have experienced when using cannabis
  • Your opinions of the current legislation in ACT around cannabis
  • Future preferences for cannabis laws
The survey should take around 10 minutes to complete.


Your answers are strictly anonymous and confidential.

You will be allocated an anonymised user ID code that in no way allows your responses to be matched to personal and/or identifying data. We do not ask for any information that might identify you.

Please keep note of your user ID and password. As the survey is anonymised, the research team will be unable to locate or reset your login details.

Once you complete the survey, you can register to have a small sample of your cannabis collected anonymously by courier and analysed for cannabinoid and contaminant content.
