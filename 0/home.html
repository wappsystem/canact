Welcome to the CAN-ACT Study

New cannabis growth and possession laws came into effect in the Australian Capital Territory (ACT) in January 2020, allowing ACT residents to grow and possess small quantities of cannabis. As researchers from the University of Sydney, we are launching an Australia-wide online survey of people residing in ACT who have prior or current experience with using or growing cannabis.

Eligibility
You are invited to participate in the study, if:
  • You currently live in the ACT; and
  • You have current or prior experience using cannabis for any reason; or
  • You have current or prior experience growing cannabis.
The CAN-ACT study involves two components:
  1. An online survey
  2. The opportunity to have your personally-grown cannabis samples analysed for cannabinoid and contaminant content


The survey asks about:
  • You: your age, gender, level of education and current living and work situations
  • Your history of cannabis use
  • Your experience with growing cannabis
  • Access to prescribed medicinal cannabis
  • Access to non-prescribed medicinal cannabis
  • Opinions of the law and current legislation
The survey should take around 10 minutes to complete.

Your answers are anonymous and confidential. You have been allocated a user ID code that in no way allows your responses to be matched to personal and/or identifying data. We will not ask any information that could identify you.

Once you complete the survey, you can register to have samples of your cannabis analysed for cannabinoid and contaminant content.
